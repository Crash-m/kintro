Polite Informal

We use the polite informal when talking with peers; most ordinary conversation will take place in the polite informal.

Now relax and take a deep breath. To figure out the correct endings for a polite informal verb, you’re going to need to make quite a few decisions. At first this is going to take a long time to figure out how to say a verb while you work through the steps. After practice, it will be almost automatic (trust me—it really will be).

Step 1

Look at the stem of the verb.

  • If it ends with 하 , change it to 해요 , and you're finished.
  • If it doesn't end with 하, proceed to step 2.

    Example

    VerbStemResult
    하다 (to do)하- 해요

Step 2

Look at the last vowel of the stem.

  • If it is a ㅏ or ㅗ , add 아요 . If you end up with two vowels right next to each other, combine them into one vowel.
  • Otherwise, proceed to step 3.

    Examples:

    VerbStemAdd 아요 Result
    가다 (to go) ᄀ​ᅡ ᄀ​ᅡ + 아요 가요
    보다 ᄇ​ᅩ ᄀ​ᅩ + 아요 봐요
    좋다 (to be good) 좋- 좋 + 아요 좋아요

Step 3

In all other cases:

  • Add 어요. Again, if you end up with two vowels next to each other, they will contract into one vowel.

    Examples:

    VerbStemAdd 어요 Result
    배우다 (to learn) 배ᄋᅟ​ᅮ 배ᄋᅟ​ᅮ + 어요 배워요
    가르치다 (to teach) 가르ᅔᅟ​ᅵ 가르ᅔᅟ​ᅵ + 어요 가르쳐요
    있다 (to have) 있- 있 + 어요 있어요

Take another deep breath, and congratulate yourself for having made it through this page, which is probably the most complicated one in the whole tutorial!
